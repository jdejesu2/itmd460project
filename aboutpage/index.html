About Us

Our Goal

Our goal is to reach out and touch the lives of the students in the 15th Ward of Chicago by helping them reach their full potential in education.

Our Story

VAMOS was founded in 2001 as S.O.L., Summer of Learning, a student-led organization, by Claudette Soto (at the time an undergrad Illinois Institute of Technology) in response to the lack of opportunities for the youth of Gage Park to enter and be exposed to STEAM fields. S.O.L. was also started to commemorate the life of Juan Manuel Flores, a gifted young man who grew up in Back of the Yards and attended Curie High School.

Juan was labeled a lost cause because he was frequently truant from school, dabbled with drugs and alcohol, and other countless biases. While these aspects are true, no one knew that when Juan would skip school he would go to the library and sit there for hours reading. He taught himself poetry, calculus, physics, arts, anything and everything he could get his hands on.

In the Fall of 1996, Juan decided to walk along the “L” tracks. He slipped and fell hitting the electrified rail. There ended all of his potential at the age of 19. Education and peer involvement were needed, but not given to Juan, an issue that occurs everyday in countless neighborhoods across Chicago. During the winter of 2002, S.O.L was changed to VAMOS (Volunteer and Mentor One Summer “In Memory of Juan”) and the component of mentorship was added. A higher level of peer involvement took place, as well as more parental involvement. VAMOS was comprised of professionals seeking to promote higher education to those who might otherwise be labeled as “lost causes”; to provide direction in short-term educational improvement and long-term college attendance. VAMOS was the next step to reaching and supporting a community who would have otherwise gone unnoticed.

In the summer of 2016, VAMOS was relaunched, and along with the same principles, a new generation of students, under the guidance of Claudette, will reach out and touch the lives of the students in the Back of the Yards neighborhood, Gage Park and Brighton Park. Our goal is to connect with students who are not the high achievers and help them reach their full potential in not only STEAM related fields and higher education, but by also embracing their Latino culture.
